# Walkthrough (complete spoilers)

This is a full 100-point playthrough, matching `tests/walkthrough.txt`.
Commands are in capitals; commentary in between. Turn-critical moments
are marked ⏱ — elsewhere you may dawdle, examine things, and talk to
whomever you please.

## Chapter I — Down the Rabbit-Hole

The White Rabbit runs by on its own schedule; let two or three turns
pass (X SISTER, X BOOK, X DAISIES) until it pops down the hole.

    FOLLOW RABBIT

Falling takes a while, and the shelves drift past:

    Z / TAKE JAR / PUT JAR IN CUPBOARD / Z

(you do not like to drop it, for fear of killing somebody underneath —
2 points for good manners). After landing:

    FOLLOW RABBIT

**The Long Hall.** The doors are all locked. Find the door:

    SEARCH CURTAIN
    TAKE KEY
    UNLOCK LITTLE DOOR WITH KEY
    OPEN LITTLE DOOR

You see the garden — and cannot fit. Wait for the draught (3 turns): the
door blows shut, the conscientious lock fastens itself, the key returns
to the table, and a DRINK ME bottle appears.

    DRINK BOTTLE

⏱ The shrink is slow — which is exactly why you can never beat the door
with the bottle. At ten inches high the key is hopelessly above you, but
a glass box has come to notice under the table:

    TAKE CAKE / EAT CAKE

Nine feet high. Now the key is easy:

    UNLOCK LITTLE DOOR WITH KEY / OPEN LITTLE DOOR

Still no use — and this is where the pool comes from:

    CRY

## Chapter II — The Pool of Tears

The Rabbit patters back; addressed (or simply approached), he bolts,
dropping his fan and gloves.

    TAKE FAN

⏱ The fan shrinks you one size per turn while held. Wait one turn (nine
feet → usual height → a foot high), then:

    DROP FAN

(3 points for the narrow escape; if you forget, it slips from your
fingers at the last instant, but no points). Your foot slips — splash.
In the pool:

    ASK MOUSE ABOUT WAY
    FOLLOW MOUSE

Do **not** mention cats, Dinah, or terriers; if you do, APOLOGIZE (or
say SORRY) and all is forgiven.

## Chapter III — A Caucus-Race

    LISTEN                (the Mouse's dry history; it does not dry you)
    TALK TO DODO          (a Caucus-race is proposed)
    RUN / RUN / RUN       (everybody has won, and all must have prizes)
    GIVE COMFITS TO CREATURES
    GIVE THIMBLE TO DODO  (we beg your acceptance of this elegant thimble)
    ASK MOUSE ABOUT TALE  (a long and a sad one; it ends in offence)
    SAY DINAH             (the party melts away)
    Z / Z                 (the White Rabbit arrives: "Why, Mary Ann!")
    EAST

    Z / Z

    Z

    Z
    KICK                       ⏱ (there goes Bill!)
    SAY DINAH                  (dead silence; then a barrowful of pebbles)

**The puppy.** You are three inches high and it is enormous.

    THROW STICK AT PUPPY
    HIDE BEHIND THISTLE        ⏱
    HIDE / HIDE                (the puppy tires; 5 points)
    SOUTH

## Chapter V — Advice from a Caterpillar

Keep your temper: be polite, be patient, and do not attack him, or he
will leave without the hint (the mushroom still works, but you must
experiment blind).

    TALK TO CATERPILLAR   (×4, through "Who are YOU?" to the poem)
    RECITE                ("You are old, Father William" — all wrong)
    TALK TO CATERPILLAR   (he leaves: one side makes you grow taller...)
    TAKE MUSHROOM         (a bit from each edge, one in each hand)
    EAT RIGHT BIT         (chin strikes foot!)
    EAT LEFT BIT          (an immense length of neck; the Pigeon)
    TALK TO PIGEON        (×3 — serpent! a likely story indeed!)
    EAT RIGHT BIT         (the neck telescopes down)
    EAT RIGHT BIT         (your usual height — 3 points)
    EAT RIGHT BIT         (a foot high: calling size for a 4-foot house)
    SOUTH

## Chapter VI — Pig and Pepper

    KNOCK                     (no sort of use, for two reasons)
    ASK FOOTMAN ABOUT DOOR    ("Are you to get in at all?")
    OPEN DOOR                 (the anti-puzzle: simply go in — 2 points)
    ASK DUCHESS ABOUT CAT     (it's a Cheshire cat, and that's why)
    Z / Z / Z                 (lullaby; then she flings the baby at you)
    TAKE EAR                  (right ear and left foot: the only workable
                               way — 5 points)
    OUT
    Z / Z                     (grunts; a very turn-up nose; it is a pig)
    PUT DOWN PIG
    TALK TO CAT               (×4: directions, madness, croquet, pig or
                               fig, and the slow vanish, grin last)
    NORTHEAST

## Chapter VIII — The Queen's Croquet-Ground

**The hall, mastered.** "Now, I'll manage better this time." You are a
foot high with the mushroom in your pocket:

    EAT LEFT BIT          (usual height: you can reach the key)
    TAKE KEY
    UNLOCK LITTLE DOOR WITH KEY
    OPEN LITTLE DOOR      ⏱ (three turns before the draught)
    EAT RIGHT BIT         (a foot high, instantly — the trick the DRINK
                           ME bottle was always too slow for)
    IN                    (the beautiful garden — 7 points)

**The garden.**

    ASK GARDENERS ABOUT ROSES  (a red rose-tree, and a mistake)
    Z                          (the procession)
    SAY ALICE                  ("My name is Alice, so please your Majesty")
    SAY NONSENSE               ⏱ (loudly and decidedly — 3 points; they're
                                only a pack of cards, after all)
    PUT GARDENERS IN FLOWERPOT ⏱ (before the soldiers find them — 5 points)
    YES                        (can you play croquet? — of course you can)

**Croquet.** Everything moves in its own regular turn, and the game
tells you the state of all four moving parts every turn. The hedgehog
unrolls every third turn; an arch is in place every second turn; they
agree on turns 6, 12, 18... The flamingo stays tucked once TUCKed, but
its head stays down only two turns after CALMing.

    TUCK FLAMINGO
    Z / Z / Z
    CALM FLAMINGO              ⏱ (on turn five)
    HIT HEDGEHOG WITH FLAMINGO ⏱ (on turn six: through the arch! 6 points)

The Cheshire Cat's head appears; the King, Queen and executioner cannot
agree whether a head can be cut off without a body:

    Z / Z
    SAY DUCHESS           (it belongs to the Duchess — 3 points)
    Z / Z / Z / Z / Z / Z (the moralling walk; the Queen's ultimatum;
                           the Gryphon, asleep in the sun)
    FOLLOW GRYPHON

## Chapters XI–XII — The Trial

    TAKE PENCIL           (Bill's squeaks; you cannot stand it — 2 points)
    Z / Z / Z / Z / Z / Z (the accusation; the Hatter's evidence; the
                           cook; and all the while you are growing)
    SAY HERE              (the jury-box goes over like a globe of goldfish)
    PUT JURORS IN BOX
    TURN LIZARD           (head upwards this time — 3 points)
    SAY NOTHING           (nothing whatever: very important. Unimportant,
                           of course, your Majesty means)
    Z                     (the unsigned verses; sentence first!)
    SAY NONSENSE          (stuff and nonsense!)
    SAY PACK OF CARDS     (5 points, the whole pack rises into the air —
                           and you wake on the bank, with your head in
                           your sister's lap)

**Final score: 100/100 — the Wonder of Wonderland.**
